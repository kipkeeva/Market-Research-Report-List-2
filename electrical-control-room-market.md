<p><p>The electrical control room market encompasses two main types: low voltage electrical control rooms and medium voltage electrical control rooms. Low voltage electrical control rooms are designed for electrical systems that operate at voltages lower than 1,000 volts. They are commonly used in residential, commercial, and industrial settings. On the other hand, medium voltage electrical control rooms are specifically built for electrical systems operating at voltages between 1,000 and 69,000 volts. They are typically used in utility substations, industrial plants, and large commercial buildings. Both types ensure the safe and reliable control and distribution of electrical power.</p></p>
